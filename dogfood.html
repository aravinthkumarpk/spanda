Try Spanda

A first run of the latest build. No prior knowledge needed — follow the steps and tick them off. ← what changed (iteration 2.1)

Spanda is a board for your real work. You spend your time on specs; agents do the work between two checkpoints that are yours. Everything you see is your live task list (the “beads” store) — Spanda is a clean window onto it, never a second copy.

The one idea: a Do initiative runs Open → Plan → Plan review → Execution → Sign-off → Execution review → Done. The two ✋ shaded steps are the only places that need you — everywhere else an agent is working. Your job is to Approve or Reject at those two gates.
First, confirm you’re on the latest build. Open spanda.onyourdevice.ai — it should land you on Today (not the Board), and the top bar should read Today · Board · Projects · Review with a More menu. If the home page jumps to the Board instead, you’re on an old build — ask for a redeploy.

Open it

0Go to https://spanda.onyourdevice.ai.

Sign in if asked (Cloudflare Access — your normal login).

You should land on Today — the change feed — not the Board. (That’s the new default landing.) The top tabs are Today · Board · Projects · Review, left-aligned, with a More menu for everything else.

Today — your daily feed

1Read Today. It’s your written daily review. On lines the pipeline marks actionable, a small green “make it work” button appears — click it to promote that line into a real task (a form opens pre-filled; review & confirm).

Expect: the promoted item becomes a real initiative on the board. No buttons yet? Your daily just hasn’t tagged any lines — that’s fine.

The Board — your current stack

2Open the Board tab. Four columns, left-to-right: To do · Doing · Review · Done. Each card shows the title and its real state tag.

Look for: an initiative the agent is checking shows “Signing off” in Doing; anything waiting on you sits in Review. The action button reads Start (not “Run”).

3Move a card by hand (drag & drop). Drag a card one column forward.

Expect: it moves and saves immediately. Dropping onto Done asks you to confirm first. A move the workflow doesn’t allow (e.g. jumping straight to Done) snaps back — by design.

Add & edit — the four task types

4Add a task. Hit + / Add. Under Task type you get exactly four: Do · Decide · Coordinate · Follow-up (no free-text type box). Pick Do, give it a title and Acceptance criteria (a Do task won’t save without them), then create.

Expect: a new Do initiative lands at Open (To do). Only Do runs the full gated lifecycle; Decide / Coordinate / Follow-up you just move by hand.

5Edit its status. Open the task → edit → the Status field offers the current state plus the valid next states. Change it and save.

Expect: a “Task updated” toast and the card moves to the new state’s column.

The two gates — where you decide

6Open the Review tab. It lists only the initiatives waiting on your decision — the Plan review and Execution review gates. (Live agent interruptions are separate — under More → Escalations.)

Expect: each row shows a ✋ Needs you pill and its state. Nothing waiting? The empty state says so.

7Open a gated initiative. Click it — its detail pane is its status page: live state, “what’s done”, any pending question, the task breakdown, and at the top your Approve / Reject controls.

Expect: Approve advances it past the gate; Reject opens a note box — your note is the instruction the agent redoes the work against. (No reject note = can’t confirm.)

Projects — the bird’s-eye view

8Open the Projects tab. Projects show as cards with a health badge and a count. On any project, click “Board →”.

Expect: the Board opens scoped to that project — only its initiatives & tasks — with a “Scoped to project … · clear” chip. Click clear to return to the full board.

What to jot down as you go

  • Did the home page land on Today (not the Board)?
  • Does a fresh Do initiative actually stop at Plan review and Execution review — i.e. it’s not running straight through on its own?
  • Do Approve / Reject work, and does a reject note stick?
  • Does the Review tab show the right things (gates), and only those?
  • Did drag-to-move, add, edit-status, and promote all save (and survive a refresh)?
  • Right vocabulary everywhere — Start (not Run), Task (not beat)?
  • Anything slow, broken, mislabeled, or ugly.
Heads-up: this is the first real browser run of the iteration 2 / 2.1 build — it passed tests & build, but the live UI hasn’t been eyeballed before you. If the Review queue, Approve/Reject, or the status-page pane misbehave, note exactly what you saw and on which screen.

That’s the tour. The things to trust: the home is Today; the Board reflects your real tasks and you move them by hand; a Do initiative is human-gated (two reviews, with an agent sign-off in between); the Review tab is your decision queue; and edit / add / promote / drag all write straight to the one task store.
